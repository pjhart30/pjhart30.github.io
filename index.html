National Parks I've Visited

Of the 59 National Parks in the United States, I've visited 13 of them, including the "Mighty Five" of Utah, Grand Canyon, Acadia, and Yosemite. Below are my six favorite parks of the 13.

Grand Canyon

Grand Canyon National Park is a jaw-droppingly vast canyon located in Northern Arizona.

Crater Lake

Crater Lake National Park is an incredibly pure lake in Oregon created by the collapsing of a volcano. No waterways feed into the lake, so the water is purely rainwater.

Acadia

Acadia National Park in Maine features amazing scenes of the rocky Eastern coastline.

Canyonlands

Canyonlands is one of the Mighty Five Utah parks. The wide open canyons remind me of the depictions of terrain dinosaurs used to walk on.

Bryce Canyon

Bryce Canyon National Park is another Utah park that features spire-shaped rock formations called hoodoos. You feel as if you're in a cave with stalagmites when looking into the canyon.

Yosemite

Yosemite National Park in central California has the best hiking trails and views of large rock formations over the prairie and forests below, of the parks I have visited
